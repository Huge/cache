    BUILDING YOUR SPV OR SPAC

    ​

    Before you put another foot forward you need to create your SPV (Special Purchase Vehicle) or some call it a  SPAC (Special Purchase Acquisition Company).

    ​

    Approaching a seller as a company will put you in a much stronger footing as opposed to approaching them as a private individual. 

    ​

    You can set your company up in whatever jurisdiction you like but make sure its in a tax efficient area if you can. For example if you are a US citizen then Delaware or Nevada has tax advantages. As a rule of thumb never incorporate in the state which you live. 

    ​

    ​

    ​

    USA STUDENTS 

    You have 3 different company structures available to you:-

    1. LLC

    2. C-CORP

    3. S-CORP

    For the purposes of this exercise an LLC is sufficient. You can upgrade at a later time to another trading style when your accountant suggests the time is right. 

    ​

    My favourite incorporation services are:-

    • Rocket Lawyer

    • Inc Corp

    ​

    As a general rule of thumb never use your home address for your company. Always use a PO-Box, registered office services, mail forwarding service. 

    ​

    For some example names to use for your new company, check-out the templates section under SPV Development where you will find lots of ideas. 

    ​

    ​

    UK STUDENTS

    You have 2 options available to you:-

    1. LTD company

    2. PLC company

     

    (1) LTD Company

    For most, a basic LTD company structure will be sufficient. You have 3 options with incorporation

    ​

    1. Directly via the UK government (basic filling costs)

    2. Via your accountant (£300 approx)

    3. Via our in house accountant (£300, email

    ​

    As a general rule of thumb never use your home address for your company. Always use a PO-Box, registered office services and or mail forwarding service. 

    ​

    (2) PLC Company

    If you have a little extra money to burn and you want the kudos of having a public company image, you can opt for a PLC company. You will need to have £50K available to pay up the share capital and obtain a trading certificate from companies house but this money can be withdrawn from the bank account once paid up. 

    ​

    Incorporation costs can be a little more expensive but you will gain instant credibility and access to an abundance of financial mechanisms and instruments. When you approach a seller they are sure to take you a whole lot more seriously! This is exactly how I, Ken Mack started!

    ​

    For some example names to use for your new company, check-out the templates section under SPV Development where you will find lots of ideas.
